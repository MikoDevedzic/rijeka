How on-chain movement reduces initial margin across all risk classes
THE CENTRAL ARGUMENT
ISDA SIMM risk weights are calibrated to the 99th percentile of 10-day market moves. The 10-day floor was set deliberately in 2013 to make bilateral derivatives more expensive than cleared — a regulatory clearing incentive. Every single reason regulators gave for the 10-day floor is eliminated by blockchain. The regulatory logic inverts.
WHY THE 10-DAY MPOR EXISTSBCBS-IOSCO 2013 RATIONALE
①
No centralised close-out facility — bilateral counterparties must negotiate termination manually, taking days
②
IM calculation disputes — different models, different Greeks, different SIMM outputs — double MPoR to 20 days when unresolved
③
Settlement T+1 / T+2 — collateral cannot be accessed until next business day, exposure accumulates
④
Legal uncertainty — netting enforceability and close-out valuation disputed during counterparty default
⑤
No intraday margin calls — exposure can accumulate 23 hours before next EOD IM call window

④
Pre-agreed smart contract terms — close-out valuation method encoded on-chain, no legal ambiguity, no dispute
⑤
Continuous smart contract monitoring — IM called every 4 hours or on threshold breach, same as CCP intraday model

DISPUTE ELIMINATION → NO MPOR DOUBLING
Under BCBS-IOSCO rules, unresolved IM disputes double the MPoR from 10 to 20 days. Risk weights scale with √MPoR — a doubling becomes a 41% IM increase. On-chain CRIF eliminates disputes because both parties read identical immutable sensitivity inputs. The 2× MPoR multiplier risk vanishes entirely.

02
ON-CHAIN CRIF → ZERO RECONCILIATION FRICTION
Both parties independently compute CRIF using their own pricers, producing slightly different sensitivities. This drives daily reconciliation workflows (AcadiaSoft, Bloomberg BCOL), excess IM held during disputes, and operational cost. Shared on-chain pricing oracle → single CRIF → identical SIMM. Reconciliation workload: zero.

TOKENIZED IM EARNS YIELD WHILE POSTED
Traditional cash IM earns OIS (SOFR ~4.3%). Tokenized T-bills or MMFs earn their natural yield (4.75–4.80%), 45–50bp above what cash IM earns. MVA = ∫IM × funding_spread × df. Yield earned on posted collateral reduces net funding spread → MVA falls 45–50bp/year on all IM balances. CFTC Letter 25-39 (Dec 2025) explicitly enables this.

CLEANER BACKTEST DATA → FEWER FALSE EXCEPTIONS
Dynamic backtest counts days where |P&L| > SIMM(1-day). During IM disputes, collateral transfer timing is messy — firms must make judgment calls about including "disputed days." These generate spurious exceptions. Blockchain: every day is clean — atomic transfer, immutable record, no ambiguity. Statistically cleaner backtests → fewer false exceptions → avoids remediation IM add-ons.

TOKENIZED COLLATERAL → REDUCED HAIRCUTS

Tokenized assets settle atomically (T+0) → settlement-time risk = zero → haircut component removed. US Treasuries: 2% haircut → potentially 1% for tokenized. Less collateral needed for same IM requirement.

THE MISSION
When Wall Street's paperwork crisis of 1968 threatened to paralyze equity markets, the Depository Trust Company solved it by dematerializing physical certificates into book entries — creating the centralised infrastructure that still clears $87 trillion in equities annually. The problem it solved was not the securities themselves but the friction between them: reconciliation, confirmation, settlement finality. Fifty years later, bilateral derivatives markets face an equivalent structural failure — not in volume but in architecture. ISDA confirmations negotiated over days. SIMM disputed between independent models. Collateral settling T+1 across correspondent chains. Regulatory reporting assembled from fragmented post-trade records.

Rijeka is not a product built on top of this infrastructure. It is its dematerialization. The ISDA Master Agreement encoded in a smart contract. SIMM computed from immutable shared sensitivities, disputes mathematically impossible. Margin called and settled atomically. Regulatory reporting embedded in the confirmation workflow itself — not assembled after the fact.

What DTC did for paper certificates, Rijeka does for the bilateral derivatives operational stack — with one structural difference that DTC could never achieve: there is no central depository. The ledger is shared. Which means, for the first time, that a regional bank in Sarajevo and a primary dealer in New York operate on identical infrastructure — not adjacent to the same system, but on the same chain. That is not disruption. That is the natural conclusion of what DTC started.
